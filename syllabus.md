## Course description

This course prepares you for capstone execution. You'll learn to gather user requirements,
analyze and design systems, plan testing, and manage a project. By the end you'll have a
vetted proposal, ready before the formal capstone course begins.

The work runs through an AI-assisted development workflow: you set up a working toolchain,
gather requirements from a real stakeholder, write a product requirements document, and run
a short Scrum sprint that ends in a sprint review.

Prerequisite: BCOR 2710 (C or higher).

## Projects and stakeholders

Every project has a real stakeholder behind it. You can bring your own by securing a capstone
internship by Week 4 (Jul 22–28); your internship sponsor becomes the stakeholder you work
with. If you don't have an internship by then, the instructor assigns you a project and
connects you with a stakeholder. Either way, that stakeholder is the person you meet with and
record for the stakeholder meeting notes deliverable.

## Your project repository

You'll work in two GitHub repositories, but not at the same time. In Week 1 you have the
tutorial repo, where you build and deploy the dashboard. Once your team forms at the end of
Week 3, you create one shared capstone project repo and add every teammate as a collaborator.
There you apply the same workflow to your real project, and it becomes the home for your PRD,
your stakeholder meeting notes, and any artifacts for the sprint review. When you turn in a
deliverable on Brightspace, you submit the link to it in the repo.

## Learning outcomes

By the end of the course you will be able to:

- Produce a vetted capstone proposal ready to carry into ISBA 4797
- Gather and document stakeholder needs and requirements
- Write a product requirements document (PRD)
- Apply Agile and Scrum methods, including running a sprint and a sprint review
- Set up and use a professional, AI-assisted development environment (Git, GitHub, Jira,
  Claude Code), and demonstrate version-control competency
- Evaluate build-versus-buy options and identify hosting resources
- Use AI tools effectively and explain the judgment behind your work

## Course policies

### AI policy

This course treats AI as a core professional tool, and you are expected to use it. You will
work with Claude Code and other AI tools throughout. Use them to brainstorm, draft, automate,
and learn as you build. Be transparent: note where AI did substantial work on an assignment.
What is graded is your thinking, your decisions, and your ability to explain and stand behind
your work. Submitting AI output you cannot explain, or leaning on AI to skip the learning,
defeats the purpose of the course.

### Meetings and participation

Your individual and team meetings are required and are where most of the coaching happens.
One-on-ones (Weeks 2–3) run 30 minutes; team meetings (Weeks 4–6) run 45 minutes. Book each
one through the shared calendar. Four of these are graded on preparation: your two one-on-ones
and your two team working meetings in Weeks 4 and 5. Come with the week's work ready to discuss.
Missing one without prior notice earns no credit for that meeting. The Week 6 team meeting is
your sprint review, graded as its own deliverable. If you need to reschedule, tell the
instructor in advance.

### Submitting work

Submit all work on Brightspace by 11:59 PM on the due date. Without a prior arrangement,
late work loses 10% per day.

### Academic honesty

Submit your own work, with proper citation of sources and clear disclosure of AI use.
Collaboration is expected on team deliverables; individual deliverables must reflect your
own effort. You are bound by LMU's Academic Honesty Policy.
